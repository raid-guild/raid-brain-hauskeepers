## The Position

Raid Guild is not just a dev shop that uses AI tools. RG is a guild that has built a **practice around agentic development** — structured, human-in-the-loop workflows where AI agents handle routine maintenance and development tasks, allowing skilled builders to operate at higher leverage.

This is a meaningful distinction from:
- Teams that use Copilot for autocomplete
- Agencies that use ChatGPT to move faster
- Projects that bolt on an AI feature

RG's approach is architectural: it shapes how codebases are structured, how documentation is written, how issues are triaged, and how maintenance is operated.

---

## The Service Offering Taking Shape

**Name (working):** AI-Assisted Maintenance Setup

**What it is:** A structured sprint that takes a legacy project from "hard to maintain" to "efficiently maintainable with agentic tooling." Includes codebase modernization, LLM-optimized documentation, AI workflow setup, and operational playbooks.

**Who it's for:** Web3 projects (and potentially beyond) that:
- Have accumulated technical debt
- Have a small or volunteer maintainer base
- Want to stay operational without a large ongoing engineering investment
- Are ready to adopt agentic tooling but don't know where to start

**What makes RG the right choice:**
- We've done it — DAOhaus is the proof of concept
- We've evaluated the tooling landscape (see `tool-evaluations/`)
- We understand both the technical and human-in-the-loop sides
- We're a guild of builders, not a vendor pushing a platform

## Key Messages

These are the 3–5 things we want any audience to come away believing after engaging with RG on this topic.

1. **Agentic development is production-ready** — not hype, not experimental. The tools exist. RG has evaluated them and deployed them.

2. **The leverage is real** — a single maintainer operating with AI-assisted workflows can sustain a system that would otherwise require a full team. We've seen it work.

3. **Structure matters** — AI agents don't just work on any codebase. You have to build for maintainability: clear architecture, LLM-optimized docs, minimal surface area. That's a skill set RG has developed.

4. **Human-in-the-loop is the design** — we're not replacing maintainers. We're giving them superpowers. Every workflow we build includes deliberate human oversight points.

5. **RG built this practice from the ground up** — this isn't a vendor pitch. It came out of guild exploration and got validated on a real client project.
