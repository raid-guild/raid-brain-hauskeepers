# Superprism Lightweight Voice Guide

## Source References

- `https://superprism.io/`
- `https://refactory.superprism.io/`

## Audience Posture

Superprism speaks to teams, communities, and operators exploring collaborative AI systems. The voice is not a hype-driven AI startup voice; it is closer to an applied R&D lab explaining infrastructure for shared context, memory, knowledge, and human-agent coordination.

## Tone

Precise, systems-minded, calm, research-forward, operator-aware.

## What To Emphasize

- Collaborative AI infrastructure, not individual productivity tools.
- Shared context across chat, meetings, docs, repositories, and agents.
- Human-agent coordination without displacing human judgment.
- Durable memory, searchable knowledge, and visible operator workflows.
- Open source, self-hostable, adaptable infrastructure.
- Refactory as a practical system that starts with Discord and extends into maintenance workflows, review, approvals, and runtime actions.

## What To Avoid

- Suggesting agents take unsupervised production control.
- Overloading the story with the full Raid Guild service package.
- Naming implementation details that are not ready for public product positioning.
- Overclaiming autonomy, magic, or instant transformation.
- Framing Refactory as just a chatbot or support bot.
