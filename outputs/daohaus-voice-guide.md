# DAOhaus Lightweight Voice Guide

## Source References

- `https://daohaus.club/`
- `https://docs.daohaus.club/`
- `https://docs.daohaus.club/community`

## Audience Posture

DAOhaus speaks to DAO members, builders, contributors, and communities using governance infrastructure to coordinate around shared values. The voice should feel community-owned and practical, with enough protocol language to honor DAOhaus history without turning every announcement into technical documentation.

## Tone

Community-centered, direct, values-led, practical, stewardship-oriented.

## What To Emphasize

- Purpose-driven governance and community-owned coordination.
- DAOhaus as open-source infrastructure for DAOs, builders, and public goods.
- The Admin App as the central hub for operating DAOs: settings, proposals, safes, and members.
- Maintenance improvements as continuity for the DAOhaus ecosystem.
- Clearer support, contribution, and maintainer paths.
- Community participation: questions, support, feedback, and contribution are welcome.

## What To Avoid

- Implying DAOhaus was broken, abandoned, or rescued.
- Over-centering Raid Guild or Superprism in DAOhaus-owned channels.
- Overpromising autonomous AI operation.
- Making the announcement feel like a vendor case study.
- Leading with internal implementation details before community impact.
- Using too much Superprism product language in DAOhaus posts.

## Example Lines

- "DAOhaus is community-owned infrastructure for purpose-driven governance, and keeping that infrastructure maintainable is part of stewarding the ecosystem."
- "The Admin App now has a clearer maintenance path, stronger documentation, and support workflows that make it easier for contributors and maintainers to keep DAOhaus moving."
- "If you use DAOhaus, build with Moloch, or want to help steward public-goods governance infrastructure, join the community and share feedback."
