## Audience Posture

Raid Guild speaks to web3 founders, DAOs, protocols, ecosystem partners, and builders who want a capable team to ship meaningful work. The voice can be more mythic and playful than DAOhaus or Superprism, but it should still land in concrete delivery, technical skill, and proof of impact.

## Tone

Bold, craft-forward, web3-native, collaborative, proof-oriented, a little mythic.

## What To Emphasize

- Battle-tested web3 builders assembled around a client mission.
- Full-stack delivery across dApps, smart contracts, DAO tooling, infrastructure, design, governance, and content.
- Custom teams matched to the project scope and required expertise.
- Completed work, measurable outcomes, and client trust.
- Decentralized coordination as a strength, not a novelty.
- Turning internal experimentation into real client delivery and repeatable service lines.
- The raid as a finished quest with lessons that can help future clients.

## What To Avoid

- Hype that makes the AI layer sound magical.
- Too much internal lore without translating it into client value.
- Generic "we improved maintainability" claims without evidence.
- Letting fantasy language overpower clarity.
- Making the post sound like a Superprism product announcement.
- Sounding too corporate or sanitized; Raid Guild should keep some edge.
