## Article Structure

### 1. Lead: The Road To Useful Automation

Open with the AI transformation problem in practical terms. DAOhaus is mature, active web3 infrastructure with a long history. The opportunity was not to bolt an agent onto a complex system and hope for the best; it was to make the system legible enough for AI assistance to become useful, governable, and safe to operate around.

Key points:

- Mature protocols often want AI leverage but carry years of software and operational complexity.
- Maintenance knowledge often lives in old repos, stale docs, Discord history, and a few contributor brains.
- AI tools can help, but only when the underlying system has a clear enough shape and enough human control points.
- The first step toward automation is not autonomy; it is making the operating surface visible, documented, and reviewable.

Proof to use:

- Years of repo, package, dependency, docs, DNS, Discord, and infrastructure history.
- Need to support protocol maintainers without assuming a large standing team.

### 2. Origin: From Dark Factory To Client Raid

Explain the Raid Guild-side arc. Dark Factory began as cohort experimentation around AI workflow automation. DAOhaus became the live proving ground where the idea had to meet real production risk and client needs.

Key points:

- Dark Factory explored agents, memory, knowledge, workflow automation, and recurring maintenance.
- DAOhaus gave the work a real client surface.
- The engagement hardened a repeatable service pattern instead of staying a prototype.

Proof to use:

- Refactory emerged as the deployable maintenance control plane pattern.
- The raid connected Codex Runtime, Prism Memory, Prism Knowledge, Discord intake, GitHub review, Railway context, and human approval gates.

### 3. What DAOhaus Needed To Automate Safely

Frame the client need respectfully and concretely.

Key points:

- Clarify active versus legacy surfaces.
- Reduce admin app codebase complexity.
- Preserve operating knowledge in durable docs.
- Define safer maintainer workflows.
- Use AI assistance without handing production to an agent.

Avoid:

- "DAOhaus was broken."
- "Raid Guild rescued DAOhaus."
- "AI now maintains DAOhaus autonomously."

### 4. What Raid Guild Delivered

Use the five workstreams as the center of the article.

#### Codebase Modernization

- Standalone Vite, React, and TypeScript admin app.
- Extraction from prior NX/Webpack/Babel monorepo.
- Local inlining of internal `@daohaus/*` packages.
- Modernized dependencies including React, TanStack Query, React Router, wagmi, viem, and RainbowKit.
- Cleaner `app/`, `features/`, and `lib/` architecture.

#### Agent-Ready Documentation

- Root README and documentation map.
- Architecture, environment, verification, glossary, and debugging docs.
- Local `AGENT.md` files near major code areas.
- Reusable AI maintainer prompt.

#### Community And Repository Surface Cleanup

- Active, deprecated, and historical repositories mapped.
- Public docs reviewed.
- DNS and hosting records reviewed.
- Discord channels consolidated around a clearer operating model.

#### Refactory Deployment

- DAOhaus-specific Refactory instance.
- Discord and Refactory intake.
- Control plane for requests, tasks, workflows, artifacts, and agent sessions.
- Codex Runtime, Prism Memory, Prism Knowledge, GitHub, and Railway context.
- Human approval gates around meaningful state and production-affecting changes.

#### SOP And Transition Readiness Packet

- Transition overview.
- System architecture.
- Repository and application inventory.
- Infrastructure, access, and dependencies.
- Operational runbooks.
- AI-assisted maintenance workflows.
- Known risks and open gaps.
- Stewardship handoff checklist.

### 5. What Changed: From Implicit Context To Automation-Ready Operations

Focus on qualitative outcomes, not metrics.

Key points:

- The active admin app became easier to reason about and deploy.
- Maintainers gained clearer docs, repo maps, runbooks, and escalation boundaries.
- AI agents received a better operating surface: simpler code, local guidance, and structured workflows.
- Community and maintainer-facing paths became easier to navigate.
- Institutional knowledge moved into durable artifacts.
- DAOhaus gained a stronger foundation for progressive automation: clearer inputs, clearer context, clearer review gates, and clearer escalation paths.

Useful line:

The most important shift was from implicit knowledge to durable operating context.

### 6. Why Progressive Automation Needs Humans In The Loop

Make this the trust-building section.

Key points:

- The credible offer is not instant full autonomy.
- Agents help with triage, investigation, context retrieval, branch preparation, implementation assistance, and reporting.
- Humans approve status transitions, branches, pull requests, merges, incidents, access changes, and production-affecting work.
- For live protocol systems, review and approval are features.

Useful line:

The agent handles groundwork; humans handle decisions.

### 7. What This Proves For Future Clients

Connect the case study to the repeatable offer.

Key points:

- Progressive automation is a service path, not a single tool install.
- Codebase simplification is AI readiness.
- Agent-ready docs are a concrete deliverable.
- SOP packets and access matrices are high-value handoff artifacts.
- A client-specific Refactory instance can preserve context and coordinate future work.

Best-fit buyers:

- Mature web3 protocols.
- DAOs preparing stewardship transitions.
- Small product teams with large maintenance surfaces.
- Ecosystem funders responsible for public-goods software continuity.
